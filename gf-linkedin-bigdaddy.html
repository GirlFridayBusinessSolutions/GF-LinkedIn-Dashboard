Stop Doing This — Immediately
🚫
Gratitude-Led Testimonial Posts
Every post that opens with "We're grateful," "We love this feedback," or "Partnership is built over time" underperforms. Your audience is a CEO or Director — they scroll for value, not warmth. These posts average 15 impressions and near-zero clicks.
Do This Instead
Open with the client's result or problem. Lead with the outcome, not the gratitude. Example: "This client's operations were running through one person. Here's what changed in 30 days."
🚫
Vague "Support" Messaging
Posts using language like "the right support," "brings clarity," "makes everything easier" consistently land flat. This is the language of a business that sees itself as interchangeable, not indispensable.
Do This Instead
Name the specific problem. "You're the bottleneck in your own business" is more powerful than "we provide the right support." Specificity earns clicks.
⚠️
Posting Videos Without Strong CTAs
Your videos get views — the Jan 20 post hit 184 views. But CTR is consistently lower on video than text posts. People watch and don't act. The call-to-action at the end of your videos is not converting.
Fix It This Way
End every video with a single, specific ask. Say: "If your team is busy but progress feels stuck, comment STRUCTURE and I'll send you something." Give them a micro-action.
⚠️
Repeating the Same CTA Every Post
"Book a call → [link]" appears in roughly 80% of posts. This trains your audience to skip the CTA. LinkedIn's algorithm also deprioritizes posts with outbound links in captions.
Rotate These Instead
Comment "STRUCTURE" to get X · Tag a leader who needs this · Save this post · What's your experience? · DM me the word CLARITY. Vary the ask every post.
The GirlFriday Winning Post Formula
The Formula Behind Your Best Posts
Reverse-engineered from your top 10 performers · Apply this every time
01
Reframe Hook
Challenge a belief your audience holds. "Most [X] problems aren't [X] problems." This stops the scroll because it creates cognitive dissonance.
02
Name the Pain
Describe the specific, physical symptom. Not "operational complexity" — say "every decision still requires your approval." They feel it.
03
Bridge to GF
One sentence connecting the pain to GirlFriday's unique position. No more than 1–2 sentences. Don't over-explain the offer.
04
Proof or Stakes
A result, stat, or consequence. "The cost isn't obvious but it compounds quietly." Stakes language drives urgency without desperation.
05
Rotating CTA
Rotate: comment word / save post / DM / link / question. Never the same CTA twice in a row. Variety trains higher response.
A/B Tests to Run This Month
Test 1: CTA Type
Run Week 1 vs 3
Hypothesis: Comment-trigger CTAs ("Comment STRUCTURE") generate more reach than link CTAs because comments boost algorithmic distribution.
Version A — Link CTA
"If your business is ready to stop running through you, book a call and let's build the structure behind it. [link]"

Version B — Comment CTA
"If this describes your business right now, comment the word STRUCTURE and I'll send you something worth reading."
Measure: Impressions + comments
📍 Watch: Does the comment CTA post get 2× more impressions? LinkedIn rewards dwell time + comments over outbound clicks.
Test 2: Post Length
Run Week 2
Hypothesis: Think Bigger posts may convert better short. Thought leadership may convert better long. Same topic, two lengths.
Version A — Long Form
"Growth stalls when leadership becomes the bottleneck. [Full 200-word explanation of the problem, the pattern, the cost, and how GirlFriday solves it.]"
Measure: Reactions + saves
Version B — Short Punch
"Growth stalls when leadership becomes the bottleneck. Not because leaders aren't capable. Because the structure around them hasn't caught up. Fix the structure."
Measure: CTR + reactions
📍 Watch: Short posts may drive more clicks. Long posts may earn more saves. Both matter — for different goals.
Test 3: Hook Structure
Run Week 3
Hypothesis: Your reframe hooks ("Most X isn't X") outperform question hooks ("Are you struggling with X?"). Let's prove it with identical topics.
Version A — Reframe
"Most marketing problems aren't marketing problems. They're coordination problems. When operations and marketing run separately, leads come in and momentum stalls."
Measure: CTR + comments
Version B — Question
"Is your marketing working harder than your results show? If you're generating leads but not converting them, the problem is probably not your content."
Measure: CTR + comments
📍 Watch: Which gets more comments AND more clicks. Winner = your default hook format going forward.
Test 4: Content Angle
Run Week 4
Hypothesis: Free-value posts (lead magnets, save-worthy carousels) grow followers faster. Thought leadership posts generate more calls. Both are needed — but at different ratios.

"Most growing businesses don't outgrow their market. They outgrow the structure that got them here. Here's what that looks like and how we fix it."
Measure: Profile visits + calls
Version B — Free Value
